## Choosing the binding — by lifetime, then VERIFY delivery

- **A self-bound trigger dies with its session.** Arm self-bind only for a
  failsafe tied to a live persistent seat, and **re-arm it at every seat
  cutover** — an archived seat's failsafe fires into nothing.
- **A standing loop wants fresh-session-per-fire** by lifetime rationale: it
  survives session archive. **BUT the platform caveat is verified the other
  way around**: observed delivery was 0-for-2 on fresh-session cron fires vs
  100% on self-bound crons and one-shot chains (2026-07-12 forensics) —
  treat fresh-session cron as **UNVERIFIED-BROKEN until a scheduled fire is
  proven in your environment**. Handle the tension explicitly: choose the
  binding by lifetime rationale, then **verify the first scheduled delivery
  actually landed** — never assume either mode works.
- **Never hardcode environment or session ids** in prompts, docs, or
  templates: the registry has been observed surfacing a DIFFERENT
  environment id than the one recorded at arm time (a load-bearing
  suspect in the fresh-session delivery failure, not cosmetic). Ids belong
  in dated records; re-read them live at use.

## Record verbatim — every create/delete call

Record every trigger create/delete call **verbatim** — id, cron, binding,
next-fire — in the same session's heartbeat/log (and the session card).
An unrecorded arm is invisible to every later session; a paraphrased one
cannot be probed against the registry.

## Re-verify at every wake — probe, never record

- **A record is a claim; the live registry is the proof.** Verify trigger
  state by listing the registry at every wake, never by reading your own
  notes forward.
- **Presence is cheap, absence is expensive:** a fresh trigger shows on the
  first page; an ABSENCE claim requires walking the registry **to
  exhaustion** before you assert it.
- **Deleted triggers may vanish with no tombstone.** Auto-disabled triggers
  stay listed with an `ended_reason` (auto-disable values are observed);
  **total absence means hard deletion, actor unknown** — a trigger recorded
  "verified live" has vanished within hours, unfired, with no audit trail
  visible agent-side.

## Scheduler health — read the fields, not the vibes

- **Wedge signature:** `enabled ∧ next_run_at < now − 15min` = an
  undelivered fire. A healthy trigger **advances `next_run_at` after each
  fire**; a `next_run_at` frozen in the past means the scheduler is not
  delivering.
- **The manual-fire trap:** a manual `fire_trigger` sets `last_fired_at`
  WITHOUT advancing `next_run_at` — **never read `last_fired_at` alone as
  scheduler health**; a hand-kicked trigger looks alive while its schedule
  stays wedged.

## Pacing — one trigger write at a time

Trigger-MCP writes are paced **sequentially, one write at a time** —
parallel multi-call writers hung reliably under load (four workers, one
night). A short `send_later` chain is a **pacemaker for a live seat only**
and ends with the seat — the cron failsafe is the dead-man backstop, not
the pacer.

## The failsafe wake pattern

On every failsafe wake, **verify the standing loop's last slot actually
delivered** (compare the loop's expected slot against the registry and the
repo's activity) — the failsafe is the only thing that can see a missed
loop fire, and without this check a miss sits in a blind window until a
human notices. Detection latency is bounded by the failsafe cadence; the
check makes it bounded instead of unbounded.

## Seat wake discipline — hygiene + the end-of-turn invariant

For a persistent seat living on a wake chain (PL-012, the autonomy
rider's seat mechanics — cite it, don't copy it):

- **Wake hygiene:** consume-before-re-arm; exactly ONE outstanding
  pacemaker tick at any time; verify the failsafe is ALIVE at every wake
  (listed, enabled, next fire in the FUTURE — re-arm it if wedged, one
  trigger write per worker); a wake with nothing to do is a SILENT
  no-op — re-arm and exit without writes.
- **End-of-turn invariant:** every turn ends with (a) work landed or
  routed, (b) exactly one future tick armed + the failsafe verified, and
  (c) the seat's heartbeat (`control/status.md`) re-stamped LAST after
  re-reading its inbox at HEAD. Ending a turn with zero armed wakes is a
  seat-killing bug.
