# The 2026-07-12 trigger-scheduler incident ("finding 7")

> Provenance: superbot `docs/eap/night-review-2026-07-12.md` @
> `8558179e6a90670ed18c778234d789c65c2b5789`; companion narrative in
> `docs/eap/anthropic-email-2-draft-2026-07-11.md` (same commit).
> Quoted passages are verbatim.

## Headline verdict (night-review TL;DR, verbatim)

> "**The batch was armed correctly; the platform trigger scheduler degraded.**
> Between ~02:30Z and ~08:00Z the Claude Code Remote scheduler silently
> dropped one-shot (`send_later`) firings and froze several cron Routines
> (`next_run_at` stuck in the past, still `enabled`). Every seat whose
> 2-hourly failsafe cron **stayed healthy** self-revived at ~08:0xZ when the
> scheduler partially recovered — **the Q-0265 dead-man doctrine worked**."

Doctrine takeaway (verbatim): "a failsafe only protects while it is *alive*".

## The three self-wake mechanisms (email finding 7, verbatim)

> "There are effectively **three self-wake mechanisms** a fleet can use today
> — one-shot `send_later` self-messages (our 15-minute pacemaker chains),
> cron Routines bound to a persistent session (our 2-hourly dead-man
> failsafes), and fresh-session-per-fire Routines (daily loops) — and on this
> night **all three failed in different ways, silently**."

## The three silent failure modes

1. **Dropped one-shots** — "Nine due one-shots were simply never delivered
   (06:12 through 08:23 UTC; each stays `enabled` with its fire time in the
   past — no error, no retry, no state change, never back-delivered)."
   Night-review timeline: 9 dropped ticks total across 5 seat sessions.
2. **Wedged crons** — "Two crons **wedged** with `next_run_at` frozen hours
   in the past while still `enabled` (a failsafe stuck at 06:06 [venture-lab],
   a daily loop at 06:08 [kit-lab] showing 'last fire: never'); others in the
   same account caught up at ~08:0x."
3. **Dropped fresh-session daily fire** — the kit-lab daily loop (`0 6 * * *`,
   fresh-session mode) "was dropped entirely: `last fire: never`,
   `next_run_at` frozen at 06:08:52"; manually re-fired 08:46Z (recovered
   ~2.7h late).

Detection signature (§4.1, verbatim): "**`enabled=true ∧ next_run_at <
now − 15min`.** Nothing alerts on this today — the 9 dropped ticks were
visible in `list_triggers` all night."

Baseline contrast (§1): "the 07-10→07-11 batch ran clean — 84/85 `send_later`
one-shots fired (trigger-registry page-3 evidence). Same arming pattern, same
cron expressions this batch." (The 84/85 figure is the night-review's own
citation to a live registry export — not re-verifiable from git.) Root cause
(§1): "platform-side scheduler degradation … Nothing agent-side changed
between the clean 07-10 batch and this one."

## The dead-man-cron failsafe (what saved the fleet)

§3.1 (verbatim): "**Failsafe crons are the load-bearing anti-stall layer
(Q-0265) — now production-proven.** Every seat whose `*/2` failsafe stayed
healthy came back on its own the moment the scheduler breathed again. The
dark seats are exactly the ones with missing (kit-lab: daily-only) or
itself-wedged (venture-lab) failsafe coverage — coverage must exist *and*
be alive."

The two dark seats: **Venture Lab** (its failsafe itself wedged at 06:06)
and **Self Improvement / kit-lab** (daily-only loop, no failsafe layer).
The lane's own words (fig-24 caption): "never run a send_later chain without
the dead-man cron."

Cross-substrate redundancy also held (§3.2): the roster regen had moved to a
GitHub Actions cron (fleet-manager PR #81) and "kept flowing **through** the
CCR scheduler outage".

## Cross-session recovery is org-refused

> "every cross-session recovery path was refused server-side —
> `fire_trigger`/`update_trigger` on a trigger bound to another session and
> `create_trigger` targeting a sibling session all return *'not enabled for
> this organization'* — so a stalled seat cannot be revived by any other
> agent, only by the scheduler healing, a manager session that happens to
> hold `send_message`, or the operator opening the session by hand (manually
> firing fresh-session triggers does work, and is how we recovered the daily
> loop 2.7h late)."

## Allowlist-not-honored reproduced (Q-0242)

> "the repair session hit Deny/Allow prompts on those trigger tools *despite
> exact allowlist entries in the repo's settings* — reproducing, with the
> operator watching, the same allowlist-not-honored behavior we logged on
> 2026-07-07 — while Routine-spawned sessions carrying per-tool grants in
> their spawn config never prompt."

§4.3: exact `mcp__Claude_Code_Remote__*` allow entries exist at superbot
`.claude/settings.json:68-79` and the hub session still prompted on
`fire/update/create_trigger`. "In an unattended session, a prompt is a
silent stall." Figures: 25a–d (2026-07-12), 31/32 (the 2026-07-07 originals).

## Serialization vs. real drop (same-day refinement, §8 Addendum ~12:00Z)

> "A Game Lab session, working from the trigger registry, split the 'dropped
> one-shot' class in two: **ticks bound to a busy session serialize and
> deliver the moment the turn goes idle** (its 09:10Z tick fired at 11:16Z at
> exactly that boundary — sound by construction; the hub session's own 10:42Z
> check-in matched the pattern, arriving as its turn ended). The
> genuinely-failed remainder: the fresh-session daily loop ('last fire:
> never' with no busy session to queue behind) and the crons with
> `next_run_at` frozen hours in the past. **No platform surface distinguishes
> queued from lost.**"

Email framing: "a queued tick and a lost tick look identical (`enabled`,
fire time in the past) on every surface." Figure: fig-35.

## Duplicate-fire stand-down (§8, verbatim)

> "the kit-lab double-fire (manual 08:46Z kick + 10:28Z scheduler catch-up)
> resolved itself with a verified zero-write stand-down by the second run."

Email version: "when a manual kick and the late scheduler catch-up
double-fired the same daily loop, the second run verified the first's
commits and stood down with zero writes: the duplicate-fire safety you'd
need for at-least-once delivery already works on our side." Figure: fig-34.
